    Clash!

    As the result of a game design class, we were given the task of designing and creating a board game. We decided to create a simple yet interesting prototype for a card game, where two different factions (or types of attack) go hand in hand, strategies need to be devised and the usage of cards need to be well thought.

    This is the first iteration of it, and contains 83 cards of which 32 are of magic and tech attack, 16 of magic and tech defense, 20 hearts and 15 creature (ability) cards.

    The game was very well received amongst the lecturers of my University and showcased in their open days.

    A walk in the park

    This game was the product of research on the mental health topic that revolves around video games and other media industries. We decided to develop a Virtual Reality (Oculus Rift) experience where the user would walk around a park and read conversations held by popular game characters and their real life equivalents. It demonstrated the wrong display of mental health due to stigmas and stereotypes that are daily included in today's industries.

    Moa VR

    Moa VR is an HTC Vive game that features New Zealand's exctinct, wingless bird: the Moa. In the game, you are the Moa's keeper and get to learn about it as it progresses. The gameplay is very short yet engaging. I was in charge of bringing UX to life, since most common practices are to be changed when developing for Virtual Reality.
